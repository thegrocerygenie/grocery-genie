# Product Overview

## Vision

Grocery Genie is a mobile app that transforms grocery receipts into three layers of value from a single scan: real-time budget awareness, longitudinal price intelligence, and ingredient-driven meal planning. One scan feeds your budget, updates your price history, and refreshes your pantry inventory.

The core insight: a parsed grocery receipt carries budget data (what you spent), market data (what things cost, where, and when), and inventory data (what's in your kitchen). Most competing products exploit only one dimension. Grocery Genie exploits all three from the same scan event.

## Strategic Objectives

- **Habitual engagement:** Budget tracking drives weekly scanning. Price trends accumulate passively. Meal suggestions give users a reason to open the app between trips. Together these cover daily, weekly, and monthly engagement frequencies.
- **Data compounding:** Every scan enriches all three feature layers simultaneously. The app becomes more valuable the longer a user stays.
- **Phased revenue growth:** Free tier validates scanning + budgeting. Premium unlocks price intelligence and household sharing. Family tier adds meal planning.

## Personas

### Primary: Budget-Conscious Household Manager

- **Archetype:** Sarah, 34, manages groceries for a family of four
- **Demographics:** 28–45, household of 2–5
- **Shopping behavior:** 2–3 stores weekly, driven by proximity and habit
- **Current tools:** Bank app for totals, mental math, abandoned spreadsheets
- **Core pain:** No item-level visibility into grocery spending. Bank feeds show "$187 at Loblaws" but not what was purchased. Cash is invisible.
- **Motivation:** "Where is my money going?" — visibility without effort
- **Engagement:** Scans on the drive home. Checks weekly summary Sunday. Monthly review with automation.
- **Willingness to pay:** $5–$8/month if it demonstrably saves more than that

### Secondary: Health-Conscious Meal Planner

- **Archetype:** Marcus, 29, wants to cook more and waste less
- **Demographics:** 25–40, lives alone or with partner
- **Shopping behavior:** Shops impulsively — buys what looks good, figures out meals later
- **Core pain:** Buys fresh produce with good intentions, throws away 30%+ because he forgets what's in the fridge
- **Motivation:** "What can I make with what I have?"
- **Engagement:** Scans after shopping, checks meal suggestions 2–3 times/week, responds to expiry notifications

### Tertiary: Price-Sensitive Comparison Shopper

- **Archetype:** Linda, 52, actively compares prices across stores
- **Demographics:** Any age, fixed or limited income
- **Shopping behavior:** 3+ stores/week based on flyer deals, mental price baselines
- **Core pain:** No data-driven way to compare actual checkout prices across stores
- **Motivation:** "Am I getting ripped off?"
- **Engagement:** Scans every receipt religiously, checks price history before trips

## Monetization

- **Free:** 20 receipts/month, basic budget tracking (8 categories), single user
- **Premium ($5.99/month):** Unlimited receipts, price intelligence, household sharing (up to 5), custom categories, trends
- **Family ($8.99/month):** Unlimited members, meal planner, shopping list, export, priority processing
- **Partnership (long-term):** Affiliate links to grocery delivery, anonymized aggregated price data licensing

## Competitive Landscape

- **Mint / YNAB / Copilot Money:** Bank-feed budgeting. No item-level detail, no price tracking, no meal planning. Receipt-level granularity is our structural advantage.
- **Basket / Flipp:** Coupon/flyer apps. Rely on retailer-provided prices, not actual checkout prices.
- **Mealime / Paprika:** Meal planning starting from recipes → shopping list. Inverted workflow from ours.
- **Fetch / Receipt Hog:** Receipt scanning for rewards/cashback. No financial intelligence or meal planning.

No existing product combines all three layers from a single receipt scan.
